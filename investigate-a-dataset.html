Conclusions¶

Dataset Description

The US census data and Gun data are independent sources of data. However, they share a common variable which is the states and dates. These Variables were essential in data cleaning to create a common ground between the two and also provide consistency for visualization.

Question that were investigated

  1. What census data is most associated with high gun per capita?
  2. Which states have had the highest growth in gun registrations?
  3. What is the overall trend of gun purchases?

Limitations

  1. For the Census dataset, there ware missing data that might contribute in the analysis accuracy, such as the five addressed colonies; District of Columbia, Guam, Mariana Islands, Puerto Rico and Virgin Islands.
  2. Missing or NaN values in the dataset, especially in 'Fact Note' column in census data
  3. Standardization of datasets. Datatypes in datasets needed change of datatype for consistent and accurate analysis.

Findings

  1. Census data that is most associated with high gun per capita.

    2. High gun per capita is calculated by state, except District of Columbia, Guam, Mariana Islands, Puerto Rico and Virgin Islands. The reason for not being included is either, their gun total was missing or zero.

    There are few weak associations between the census variables and gun per capita: The positive association between gun per capita and variables which includes: White alone, percent, July 1, 2016, (V2016) Persons 65 years and over, percent, April 1, 2010 owner-occupied housing unit rate, 2011 -2015 The negative association between gun per capita and variables which includes: 2011-2015 Asian alone, percent, July 1, 2016, (V2016) Foreign born persons, percent, 2011-2015 Median gross rent, 2011-2015.

  2. States that with the highest growth in gun registrations

    3. Top 5 states with highest growth in gun regesistration between 2010 and 2016

    • Indiana with 71.437627% growth
    • Delaware with 67.979528% growth
    • Illinois with 67.942720% growth
    • Louisiana with 67.432969% growth
    • Florida with 65.821673% growth

    Uttah and Virginia had a decline in gun registration with a 521.257383% and 1.292055% decrease respectively

  3. The overall trend of gun purchases

    4. Most purchased guns in 2017 are:

    • Handgun with 5231079 as the number of purchase
    • Long gun with 3419092 as the number of purchase
    • Others with 276527 as the number of purchase
    • Multiple with 169062 as the number of purchase

    There is an increasing trend of gun purchases based on the line chart. In 1998 and 2015, there's a sudden increase in gun purchase. However, in 2016 the decrease in gun purchase is swift due to data collection of only 9 months in that year.
